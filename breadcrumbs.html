THE LIGHT DIMS IN CAMBRIDGE

by Hansel & Gretel Gerwig

The Light Cinema has hit back at accusations that the company has systemically ignored the many glaring issues of their Cambridge site, with the CEO dismissing these claims as “nonsense”.

“The idea that I am more concerned with any of the seven shiny new sites we have opened since the last refurbishment at Cambridge in 2017 is frankly appalling,” he begun. “It might seem like I’m opening new sites to tap funding from local councils in an attempt to grow a heavily indebted business in a volatile industry while ignoring the very sites that put the brand on the map - but that couldn’t be further from the truth. Our £40 million in annual revenue simply has better places to go.”

The company underwent an ownership shake-up in October 2024 when hospitality mogul Luke Johnson acquired the chain, pledging at least £15 million to expand the business. Despite doubling the total number of sites since Cambridge's last refurbishment now eight years ago, it is yet to be seen if the branch in question will see a penny of this generous investment.
