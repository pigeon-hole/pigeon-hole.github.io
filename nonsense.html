NEW TECHNOLOGY BRINGS HOPE TO TIRED WORKERS

by someone or other

Workers at the Light Cinema were reportedly delighted this week as plans for a “100,000 year torture pod” were unveiled.

Inspired by the Netflix series Black Mirror, Lead Designer and part-time Pigeon-Hole contributer Alan Nosferatu created the pod to be capable of distorting time for the individual entombed so that one minute in real time is perceived and experienced as 100,000 years.

“I would’ve preferred something harsher,” sighed one employee, “but at least it’s something.”

Nosferatu recalls the moment he decided to create the machine, “When I walked into a screening of A Minecraft Movie and grown men were throwing handfuls of popcorn into the air and screaming at the top of their lungs at the screen, I knew. These people had to suffer.”

“As I attempted to sweep impossibly small bits of popcorn off a decades old carpet with a matted brush, the idea just hit me.”

Executives at the Light Cinema have hailed the pod as a huge success, with 147 individuals have been subjected to the 100,000 Year Pod and experts warn that if people don’t start behaving themselves in screens that number is only bound to grow.
