The Projectionist's Curse

by Staff Writer

The hush of the auditorium is broken only by the rhythmic clatter of the projector, a sound that reverberates through the walls like a warning. Last Tuesday, it came to a stop. Room 3 went dark. The film had not snapped — the world had.

Management insisted it was a “technical issue.” But the audience, restless and damp, knew better. Someone — or something — had been seen in the booth. Again.

“It always flickers at the same time,” said a customer, voice trembling, “just as the train hits the tunnel.”
